📄 International Journal of Innovative Research in Technology (IJIRT)

Multimodal Artificial Intelligence for Brain Tumor Prediction: Bridging Accuracy and Explainability

📅 October 2025  
Abstract

Brain tumor diagnosis using medical imaging has gained significant support from Artificial Intelligence (AI), particularly deep learning models. However, existing approaches often focus on single imaging modalities, limiting diagnostic reliability and interpretability. This paper proposes a multimodal AI framework that integrates MRI sequences, radiomic features, and clinical metadata for brain tumor prediction. To address concerns of transparency and trust, Explainable AI (XAI) techniques are incorporated to visualize and justify model predictions. Experiments on publicly available MRI datasets demonstrate improved diagnostic accuracy and higher clinician acceptance compared to single-modality baselines. The findings highlight the importance of multimodal learning in capturing tumor heterogeneity and underscore the role of XAI in ensuring accountability in clinical practice.

Intelligent Traffic System (ITS)

📅 November 2023  
Abstract

One of the main issues of the modern day is effectively monitoring traffic. The influence on automobiles has increased in step with the population's exponential growth during the past century. The current transportation systems are under a great deal of strain as a result of this. Several traffic systems still operate today, ignoring the amount of traffic at that intersection, by assigning set timeslots to each traffic light. Millions of people lose valuable time in traffic as a result, while noise and air pollution increase. Our research suggests an intelligent traffic management system based on the Internet of Things (IoT) that can be used for real-time traffic monitoring and management.

📄 Gradiva Review Journal

Automated Text Document Classification Using Predictive Network

📅 April 2023  
Abstract

The automatic text classification methods are supposed to retrieve the RI (Relevant Information) as well as organize it according to its degree of relevancy with the given query. The main problem in organizing is to classify which document is relevant and which is not. Automated text classification consists of automatically organizing clustered data. We propose an automatic method of text classification using TF-IDF, WordNet word embedding algorithm, and K-means clustering machine learning algorithm. We use the WordNet word embedding algorithm to eliminate the ambiguity of words so that each word is replaced by its meaning in context. The closest ancestors of the senses of all the words in a given document are selected as classes for the specified document.

Information Extraction Using by Big Data

📅 March 2018  
Abstract

The promise of data-driven decision-making is now being recognized broadly through "Big Data." However, there is currently a wide gap between its potential and its realization. The problems start during data acquisition, where the data tsunami requires ad-hoc decisions about what to keep and how to store it reliably. Transforming such content into a structured format for semantic content and search is a major challenge addressed in this paper.

📄 International Journal of Advanced Research in Computer Science and Software Engineering (IJARCSSE)

Enhanced Translating Relational Queries into Spreadsheets

📅 April 2016  
Abstract

Spreadsheets are among the most commonly used applications for data management and analysis, yet they lack sufficient analysis for complex computation. In this paper, we determine that a spreadsheet can implement all data transformations definable in SQL simply by utilizing spreadsheet formulas. We provide a query compiler which translates any given SQL query into a worksheet of the same definition, including NULL values. Thereby, database operations become available to users who do not want to migrate to a database, allowing them to define queries in a high-level language and execute them in a plain vanilla spreadsheet.

📄 Journal of Computer, Internet and Network Security (Mantech Publications)

Descriptive Clustering of Documents on the Basis of Predictive Network

📅 2020
Abstract

With the mass of data obtainable, merely finding the information is not the only assignment of automatic text classification methods. Instead, they are supposed to retrieve and organize information according to the degree of relevancy. The main problem is classifying which document is relevant. We propose an automatic method of text classification using Machine Learning based on the disambiguation of meaning. We use the WordNet word embedding algorithm to eliminate word ambiguity so that each word is replaced by its meaning in context. This facilitates more accurate categorization based on the semantic senses of the words within the documents.
